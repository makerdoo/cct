Instructing

The performing arts are about much more than simply perfoming. Portraying character is more than pretending to be the character, it entails understanding why the character would make the choices he or she makes. As such it is an intro to psychology in the same way that learning to dance provides a gateway to a lifetime of coordination and fitness.

Likewise, vocal instruction focuses on articulation, breath control, posture and numerous other disciplines that carry a lifetime of benefits including increased confidence, a firm foundation upon which one might be able to engage in public speaking, improved posture, and improved circulation due to more complete oxygenation in the blood stream.

What do theatre kids learn? You might be surprised to learn that the primary things kids learn in a children's theater are life skills like Teamwork, Problem Solving, Focus, and the Ability to Meet Deadlines. Creating Character Together


PRE PRODUCTION: HAMLET (Part One)

Description:

    Ever thought you might want to direct a show? In this course we will go through the basic steps that directors take before they even cast a production. Through script analysis, set planning, writing a costume plot and character descriptions students will learn why over three hundred and eighty years after his death, William Shakespeare's Hamlet remains one of the most challenging tragic heroes of all time. The story of Hamlet has been used as the base to such movies as The Bad Sleep Well, Strange Brew, and even Disneys The Lion King.
    During this 5 week course we will begin the pre production process with script analysis.Part Two and Three of this course will follow as we continue the process of preparing a show for production. Taking AP English? This course can give you an edge as it is one of the subjects that is used in that class.
